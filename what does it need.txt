Expense and Income Tracking: Provide a user-friendly interface for users to input and 
track their expenses and income. 
Users should be able to categorize transactions, specify amounts, and add relevant 
details such as dates and descriptions.

Budget Planning: Allow users to set up budget plans for different categories 
(e.g., groceries, transportation, entertainment).
Users should be able to define spending limits and track their progress towards 
budget goals.

Transaction Management: Enable users to view and manage their transactions, including editing, deleting, and adding new entries. The ability to sort, filter, and search transactions is also helpful.

Expense Visualization: Provide visual representations of spending patterns and budget progress, such as charts, graphs, or reports. This allows users to gain insights into their financial habits and make informed decisions.

Notifications and Reminders: Implement features to notify users of approaching budget limits, payment due dates, or irregular spending patterns. This helps users stay on track and avoid overspending.

Data Export and Import: Allow users to export their budget data in common file formats (e.g., CSV, Excel) for backup or analysis purposes. Additionally, provide the ability to import data from external sources, such as bank statements or expense tracking apps.

Goal Setting: Enable users to set financial goals, such as saving targets or debt reduction plans. Display progress towards these goals and provide actionable insights or suggestions to help users achieve them.

Multiple Accounts or Budgets: If desired, offer support for managing multiple accounts or budgets. This can be useful for users who want to track finances separately for different purposes (e.g., personal budget, business budget).

Mobile-Friendly Design: Ensure that the Budget Tracker is responsive and accessible on various devices, including smartphones and tablets. A mobile app or a responsive web design allows users to manage their budget on the go.
